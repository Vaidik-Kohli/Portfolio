About

I started with Python because I wanted to make something move on screen, not just print lines in a console. That led me into Pygame, then into web work when I needed somewhere to document what I was building.

Most of what I know came from breaking things. I read docs, test ideas, and write down what a bad decision taught me. That habit moved me faster than any course did.

Cricket, photography, and games take up most of my time outside code. All three teach you something about pacing and feedback — turns out that transfers.
