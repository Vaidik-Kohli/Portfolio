Design Tips for Developer Portfolios

Design Portfolio UI/UX

Your portfolio is often the first impression you make. Here's what I've learned about designing one that actually stands out — without being a professional designer.

1. Less Is More

The biggest mistake I see in developer portfolios is trying to show everything at once. Flashy animations, multiple fonts, rainbow colors — it all competes for attention and ends up feeling chaotic. The best portfolios are clean, focused, and breathable.

Give your content room. White space (or in our case, dark space) isn't empty — it's intentional. It directs the viewer's eye and makes your content easier to digest.

2. Pick a Color System and Stick With It

You don't need ten colors. You need:

  • A background color (dark or light)
  • A primary text color for headings
  • A secondary text color for body text
  • An accent color (or gradient) for CTAs, links, and highlights

That's it. Four colors can carry an entire portfolio. Use CSS custom properties so you can tweak everything from one place.

3. Typography Hierarchy Matters

Pick one font family with multiple weights. I use Inter — it's clean, modern, and has weights from 300 to 800. Then establish a clear hierarchy:

  • Hero heading: Large, bold (700-800 weight)
  • Section headings: Medium, semi-bold (600-700)
  • Body text: Regular weight (400), generous line-height (1.7+)
  • Small text: Lighter (300-400), muted color
"Good typography is invisible. Bad typography is everywhere." — Oliver Reichenstein

4. Animate With Purpose

Animations should guide attention, not distract from it. A simple fade-up on scroll is more effective than a 3D rotating card that takes 2 seconds to settle. I use IntersectionObserver with a subtle translateY and opacity transition — it's lightweight, smooth, and doesn't annoy visitors.

5. Mobile First, Always

More than half of web traffic is mobile. If your portfolio doesn't look good on a phone, you're losing visitors. Start designing for small screens, then scale up. A hamburger menu, single-column layouts, and properly sized touch targets are non-negotiable.

Final Thought

Your portfolio doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to be yours. Show your personality, demonstrate your skills, and make it easy for someone to get in touch. That's what matters.
